// SEE THE CLIMB BEFORE YOU WALK IT

The point of the profile is knowing where you are on the climb. Your live position rides the line, named landmarks float above it, and the readout tells you how far above or below the planned ground you actually stand. One-way, loop, or out-and-back — and loops wrap continuously, so you can pan past the start and keep going.

Elevation Profile PREVIEW

Ground elevation comes from lidar, never from consumer GPS, so the line is the terrain and not your receiver's guesswork. Fetch elevation and landmarks at home over wi-fi; the whole profile then works offline.

I’ve worn a lot of hats out there—from biological field tech and surveyor to wildfire lookout, have worked in habitat conservation, environmental education and green space and trail maintenance. I’m also a backcountry traveler with over thousands of miles spent hiking, paddling, climbing, working and living off the grid. I’ve experienced what makes field essential tools work well and what just gets in the way. Building on those lessons, my primary goal is developing straightforward, offline-capable tools designed for the practical realities of navigating and collecting data in the wild. When the tools work in the backcountry, we know they’ll easily work in side- and front-country contexts too.
